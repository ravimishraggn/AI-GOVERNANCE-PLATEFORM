# Vision and Mission

## Vision

Every AI system deployed on the Private Markets AI Platform is trustworthy by design —
not by hope, manual review, or after-the-fact investigation. Trustworthy means: governed,
auditable, compliant, and in control of the people who are accountable for it.

## Mission

The AI Governance Platform exists to answer the governance questions that no individual
application team can answer alone, at the scale of 100+ teams, thousands of agents, and
billions of inferences — without becoming a bottleneck.

---

## Why This Platform Exists

### The Regulatory Reality

Private Markets firms operate under fiduciary obligations, model risk management
requirements (SR 11-7), books-and-records rules (SEC Rule 17a-4), and an accelerating
wave of AI-specific regulations. An AI system that produces a hallucinated valuation comp,
a biased risk score, or an undocumented investment recommendation is a regulatory event,
not just a product bug.

Without a governance platform, each application team builds its own compliance controls
inconsistently, produces its own audit trails incompletely, and answers its own regulatory
questions expensively. The enterprise progressively loses coherence.

### The Scale Problem

Manual governance does not scale beyond a handful of AI systems. At 100+ teams and
thousands of agents, human review of every deployment becomes a bottleneck that slows
the business and incentivizes shadow AI — ungoverned AI running on personal API keys
outside platform visibility.

Governance must be automated, programmatic, and self-service. That requires a platform,
not a process.

### The Coordination Problem

A prompt change in one workflow may affect risk scores across ten applications.
A model version upgrade may require re-certification across fifty agents.
A new regulation may require changes across two hundred deployments.

Only a platform that sees the entire dependency graph can reason about these cross-system
implications. Individual teams cannot.

## Enterprise Value Proposition

### Trust Infrastructure
Governance makes AI systems trustworthy by design. Trust is not a feature of any single
application; it emerges from the governance layer that underlies all of them.

### Regulatory Capital
Firms with demonstrable AI governance attract institutional capital, pass LP due diligence
faster, and operate in jurisdictions where weaker competitors cannot. Governance is
a competitive moat, not just a cost of compliance.

### Platform Leverage
Every dollar invested in the Governance Platform is amortized across hundreds of
applications. A centrally built policy engine, deployed once, governs every AI system.
The marginal cost of governing the 101st application approaches zero.

### Velocity Enablement
Teams that would spend weeks building their own compliance controls consume governance
as a service in days. Self-service governance is a velocity multiplier for the business.

### Risk Capital Preservation
The expected cost of a governance failure — regulatory fines, reputational damage,
client defection, litigation — vastly exceeds the cost of this platform.

## Why Governance Is a Platform, Not a Feature

Governance becomes a platform rather than a feature when three conditions hold simultaneously:

**Scale Condition**: AI systems exceed the capacity for manual review. At 100+ teams and
thousands of agents, governance must be automated.

**Heterogeneity Condition**: AI systems vary enough in type, risk, and regulatory context
that a single approach cannot apply uniformly. The platform must be composable.

**Coordination Condition**: Governance decisions in one part of the platform have
dependencies in others. Only a platform can reason about these dependencies.

All three conditions hold in this architecture from day one. Retrofitting governance onto
a platform built without it is the most expensive mistake an enterprise AI team can make.
