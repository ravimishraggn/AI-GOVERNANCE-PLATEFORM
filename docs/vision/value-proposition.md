# Enterprise Value Proposition

## Primary Stakeholders and What They Gain

### Chief AI Officer / Chief Technology Officer
- Complete real-time inventory of every AI system in the enterprise
- Platform-level cost attribution and budget governance across all AI workloads
- Evidence-based answers to board-level AI risk questions
- A single governance control surface instead of 100 team-specific approaches

### Chief Risk Officer
- Quantified AI risk posture across the entire platform with drill-down capability
- Risk threshold enforcement that does not depend on team self-discipline
- Behavioral monitoring that detects risk changes before they become incidents
- Audit-ready documentation for every registered AI system

### Chief Compliance Officer
- Continuous compliance posture tracking against SEC, FINRA, GDPR, AIFMD, and others
- Automated evidence package generation for regulatory examinations
- Regulatory change impact assessment: "which of our 2,000 agents does this new rule affect?"
- Compliance exceptions tracked with justification, approver, and expiry

### Product Teams and Engineers
- Self-service governance in minutes, not weeks of manual process
- Clear, specific feedback on what governance requirements apply to their artifact
- Governance SDK that embeds controls without requiring engineers to become compliance experts
- No redesign required when governance requirements evolve — SDK updates propagate automatically

### Enterprise Customers (Tenants)
- Tenant-specific governance configuration matching their own regulatory profile
- Complete isolation: their governance data, policies, and audit trails are exclusively theirs
- Governance posture reporting they can show to their own LPs and regulators
- Confidence that the SaaS platform they are consuming is itself governed to enterprise standards

## Return on Investment Model

### Cost Avoidance
| Category | Estimated Annual Cost Without Platform | Notes |
|----------|---------------------------------------|-------|
| Regulatory examination preparation | $500K – $2M | Manual evidence compilation across 100+ systems |
| AI incident investigation | $200K – $1M per incident | Forensic reconstruction without audit trails |
| Duplicate governance engineering | $2M – $5M | Each team building its own compliance controls |
| Policy drift remediation | $1M – $3M | Audit finding remediation and rework |
| **Total avoidance (conservative)** | **$4M – $11M/year** | Scales with number of teams and AI systems |

### Revenue Enablement
- Governance posture as a differentiator in enterprise sales cycles
- Faster onboarding of regulated enterprise customers (governance already in place)
- Access to LP segments that require AI governance evidence

### Platform Efficiency
- 70–80% of deployments automated through Tier 1 clearance, eliminating weeks of manual review
- New teams reach production governance posture in days instead of months
- Governance policy changes propagate automatically — no manual updates across 100 teams

## Benchmark Comparisons

The governance capability described here is equivalent in scope to:
- Microsoft Azure AI Content Safety + Azure Policy + Microsoft Purview (combined)
- AWS Bedrock Guardrails + AWS Config + AWS CloudTrail AI extensions
- The internal governance platforms at BlackRock Aladdin, Bloomberg Terminal AI.

The differentiator is that this platform is purpose-built for Private Markets domain
requirements — valuation governance, MNPI controls, fund-level compliance — rather than
adapted from generic cloud provider governance tools.
